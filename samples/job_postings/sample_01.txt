Senior Software Engineer — Platform Infrastructure
Meridian Financial Technologies | Austin, TX (Hybrid)

About Meridian

Meridian Financial Technologies builds the compliance and risk infrastructure that powers mid-market investment firms across North America. Our platform processes over $40B in transactions annually and is subject to SEC, FINRA, and SOC 2 Type II requirements. We're a 180-person company that has grown 3x in the past two years, and the engineering team is expanding to keep pace.

This isn't a growth-stage startup with a ping-pong table and vibes. We ship software that has to be correct. Our on-call rotation is real, our incident reviews are blameless but rigorous, and our engineers are expected to own their systems end-to-end — from schema migrations to runbook updates.

The Role

We're looking for a senior engineer to join the Platform Infrastructure team. You'll work primarily on the internal developer platform — the CI/CD pipelines, deployment tooling, secrets management, and the service mesh that roughly 60 engineers depend on every day.

About 40% of your time will be Kubernetes: writing controllers, debugging scheduling issues, tuning resource quotas. Another 30% is Python — we have a large internal tooling surface written in Python 3.11+, and we're gradually migrating older scripts to proper services. The remaining time is architectural: working with team leads on capacity planning, dependency audits, and the occasional greenfield service.

This role is not a pure SRE role. You will write production code. You will also get paged at 2am if something you deployed causes a P0.

What We Require

Candidates must have at least 7 years of professional software engineering experience. Specifically:

- Strong Python fluency (3.8+). Not scripting-level — we mean writing testable, maintainable services with proper packaging, dependency management, and CI.
- Production Kubernetes experience. You should be comfortable with kubectl, Helm charts, and custom controllers. CKA certification is not required but suggests you know the material.
- Familiarity with distributed systems concepts: consensus, idempotency, backpressure, at-least-once vs exactly-once delivery.
- PostgreSQL — query optimization, index design, understanding of MVCC.
- Experience operating in a regulated environment (SOC 2, PCI, HIPAA, or similar).
- Strong communication skills. We do a lot of async writing (RFCs, design docs, ADRs) and expect engineers at this level to lead technical discussions, not just participate.

What Would Help (But Isn't Required)

If you have experience with any of the following, mention it — it'll make your application stand out:

- Rust. We're not a Rust shop yet, but we have one service written in it and would love someone who can grow that footprint.
- Apache Kafka or other event streaming platforms (we use Kafka for our audit log pipeline).
- Terraform or Pulumi for infrastructure as code.
- Previous work at a financial services company or fintech.
- Experience building internal developer portals (Backstage or similar).

Compensation and Logistics

This is a full-time, permanent position. We offer a hybrid schedule: engineers are expected in our Austin office on Tuesdays and Thursdays. Remote candidates outside Austin will not be considered for this particular role.

Salary range: $155,000–$195,000 base, depending on experience. We also offer equity (0.05–0.15% ISO options on a 4-year vest with a 1-year cliff), a 4% 401k match, and fully employer-paid health, dental, and vision for employees and dependents.

The Interview Process

1. 30-minute recruiter screen
2. 60-minute technical screen with two platform engineers (systems design and a live debugging exercise)
3. Take-home: a small Kubernetes controller task (we pay $200 for your time regardless of outcome)
4. Final round: three 45-minute interviews with senior engineers and the engineering manager

We aim to complete the process in under three weeks. We don't do whiteboard leetcode. The take-home is the hardest part.

To apply, send your resume and a brief note about a distributed systems problem you've debugged or designed around to careers@meridianft.com. Generic cover letters will be ignored.
